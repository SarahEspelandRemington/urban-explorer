We should evolve the app architecture toward Explore becoming the natural home view, rather than treating the opening screen as a permanent “mode chooser.”

Please revise the current entry/onboarding flow accordingly.

Core product direction
The app should feel like:

* “you are already somewhere interesting”
  NOT:
* “choose which subsystem you want to enter.”

Explore is becoming the default spatial home of the app.
Walk is becoming a deeper listening/guided mode launched from Explore.

This is NOT a major redesign.
This is a refinement of hierarchy, flow, and conceptual clarity.

Please implement the following:

1. App launch behavior

Instead of centering the experience around a large opening chooser screen:

* launch directly into Explore mode/home
* show the map immediately
* show nearby discoveries immediately
* preserve current location/search capability
* preserve the calmer navy visual system already implemented

The app should feel immediately alive and geographically grounded.

2. Replace the current full-screen entry screen with a lightweight introductory/home treatment

Do NOT show the current giant stacked-card chooser as the first full screen anymore.

Instead:

* integrate the emotional orientation directly into Explore/home
* the app itself becomes the onboarding

At the top of Explore/home, incorporate:

Title:
“Explore”

Supporting line:
“Small stories hidden in ordinary places.”

Tone:
quiet, thoughtful, grounded.

3. Reposition Walk as a contextual action

Walk should no longer appear as an equal top-level conceptual product beside Explore.

Instead:

* Walk becomes a contextual action launched from Explore/home
* users first orient visually/spatially
* then decide whether to begin listening or planning a walk

This is important because:

* Explore builds trust
* Walk depends on that trust

4. Add a clear Walk entry point INSIDE Explore/home

Add a lightweight but visible action somewhere appropriate in Explore/home for entering Walk.

Examples (choose whichever fits best with the existing architecture):

* “Start listening nearby”
* “Go for a walk”
* “Walk with audio”
* similar language

This should open the Walk landing/options screen.

IMPORTANT:
Do NOT auto-start wandering narration immediately anymore.

5. Walk landing screen architecture

The Walk landing/options screen should become the intentional chooser for:

* Wander Walk
* Planned Walk
* future guided/curated walks

Current issue:
Users tap Walk and are immediately dropped into Wander mode, while planned walks are hidden behind an additional screen after ending the walk.

Please fix this flow now.

6. Preserve what is already working visually

Keep:

* deep navy palette
* restrained glow
* calmer map treatment
* softened marker language
* cleaner hierarchy
* editorial warmth
* reduced clutter

Avoid:

* reintroducing loud onboarding
* giant CTA imbalance
* utility-app permission vibes
* overcomplicated transitions

7. Clarify Explore vs Walk conceptually

The mental model should become:

Explore:

* visual
* map-first
* spatial discovery
* browsing nearby stories

Walk:

* listening
* guided movement
* ambient narration
* planned or wandering experiences

Users should understand this naturally from the architecture itself, not from heavy explanatory copy.

8. Acceptance criteria

After implementation:

* the app opens into a calm, trustworthy Explore/home state
* the map feels like the natural center of the product
* Walk feels like a deeper mode launched intentionally
* users can access planned walks without first entering Wander mode
* onboarding friction is dramatically reduced
* the app feels more like one coherent companion experience rather than multiple disconnected modes

Do NOT redesign the entire app.
This is an architectural and hierarchy refinement pass.
