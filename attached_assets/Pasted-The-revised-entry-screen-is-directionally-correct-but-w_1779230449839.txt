The revised entry screen is directionally correct, but we need to simplify the hierarchy and align it with the actual product structure.

Goals:

* calmer
* clearer
* less “mode picker”
* more emotionally grounded
* better aligned with how Walk actually works

Please revise the entry screen as follows:

1. Replace the top section title

Change:
“Location Access”

To:
“Discover your surroundings”

Add the supporting line beneath it:
“Small stories hidden in ordinary places.”

Tone:
quiet, thoughtful, inviting — not marketing-heavy.

2. Reduce the visual weight of the search/location section

The current top card dominates the screen too much.

Please:

* reduce its vertical height significantly
* make it feel like lightweight utility/navigation
* preserve:

  * search field
  * current location behavior
  * “Explore this location” CTA
* but visually subordinate it to the two main choices below

This should no longer feel like a permissions/search gateway.

3. Clarify Explore vs Walk

Replace the current card copy with:

EXPLORE
Headline:
“See stories around you.”

Supporting text:
“Browse nearby discoveries on the map.”

WALK
Headline:
“Listen as you wander.”

Supporting text:
“Choose a guided walk or wander freely.”

This is important:
Walk should no longer imply ONLY immediate ambient wandering mode.

4. Fix the Walk entry flow

Current issue:
Tapping Walk immediately starts Wander Walk Mode, while planned/guided walks are hidden behind a second internal screen after ending the walk.

Please change this architecture:

Tapping Walk should first open the Walk landing screen.

That screen should become the intentional chooser for:

* Wander Walk
* Planned Walk
* Guided/curated walks (future-friendly)

Do NOT auto-start wandering audio immediately from the home screen anymore.

5. Adjust visual hierarchy

The Explore and Walk cards should remain peer options, but:

* Explore should feel slightly more immediately approachable
* Walk should feel slightly calmer/more intentional

Do this subtly:

* brightness
* surface contrast
* icon treatment
* spacing

Avoid giant orange CTA imbalance.

6. Preserve overall direction

What is working:

* calmer navy palette
* cleaner hierarchy
* reduced visual clutter
* more cohesive feeling with Walk Mode
* softer atmospheric tone

Do NOT redesign the whole screen.
This is refinement and clarification, not a new concept.

Acceptance criteria:

* the screen feels intentional rather than instructional
* users understand Explore vs Walk immediately
* Walk no longer accidentally hides planned walks behind a wandering-only default
* the screen feels like the beginning of one coherent product experience
* hierarchy is calmer and easier to scan
