This direction is substantially better and much closer to the correct architecture.

Please implement the revisions discussed previously, plus the refinements below.

Most important additional feedback:

The app now feels coherent and elegant, but it still does not fully feel like Streetlit yet.

Right now it risks feeling like:

* a beautifully designed urban discovery app
  instead of:
* a calm companion helping you notice hidden layers of place.

Please use the following guidance while implementing.

1. Move the Walk CTA banner ABOVE the discovery list

Current placement inside the feed slightly reads like a sponsored/promotional card interrupting discoveries.

Reorder to:

* location/context header
* Explore title
* “Small stories hidden in ordinary places.”
* radius chips
* Walk CTA banner
* discoveries list

Keep the Walk banner visually restrained and integrated into the system.

2. Add one subtle “anchor” discovery treatment

The Explore list risks becoming visually flat because every card currently has identical hierarchy.

Please add ONE subtle focal treatment to the nearest/most relevant discovery.

Examples:

* slightly larger card
* softer glow
* richer surface contrast
* slightly expanded spacing
* subtle “nearby” treatment

Do NOT turn this into a loud featured article.
The effect should be atmospheric and calm.

3. Revise Wander copy

Change:
“Put your phone away and wander.”

To something softer and more trust-building.

Preferred direction:
“Listen as you wander.”

Supporting text can remain similar:
“Stories narrate automatically as you move through the neighborhood.”

Important:
Do not imply fully screenless behavior yet.
Users still need spatial trust-building.

4. Guided Walks card should feel aspirational, not disabled

The current “SOON” treatment reads slightly dead/unavailable.

Please make it feel:

* future-facing
* intriguing
* part of the world of the app

Possible approaches:

* softer opacity reduction
* “Coming soon”
* subtle example themes:

  * Hidden Infrastructure
  * Jazz Age Midtown
  * Waterfront Industry
  * etc.

The feeling should be:
“more experiences are coming”
not:
“feature disabled.”

5. Preserve Walk tab behavior

The Walk tab should continue opening the Walk landing/chooser screen.

Do NOT revert to immediate auto-start narration.

6. Important emotional/tone guidance

The app should increasingly feel like:

* urban field notes
* layered memory
* hidden traces of ordinary life
* calm discovery
* spatial intimacy

It should feel less like:

* recommendations
* tourism
* city guides
* “top things nearby”

The visual system is already calmer and more cohesive.
Now the app needs slightly more soul and specificity in how it frames discovery.

The map, copy, and hierarchy should support:
“noticing”
rather than
“consuming content.”

7. Preserve restraint

Do NOT overcorrect by adding:

* dramatic illustrations
* excessive glow
* heavy branding
* ornamental typography
* clutter
* marketing copy

Streetlit should feel:
quietly distinctive.
