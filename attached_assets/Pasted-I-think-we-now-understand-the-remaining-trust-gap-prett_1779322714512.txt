That is a coherent and believable failure path.

   The named-street prose/location check should apply universally, including when coordSource exists.

That loophole should close.

Desired behavior:
Named-street references that are not strongly corroborated should default to INTERPRETIVE_OVERLAY instead of trusted narration candidates.

   For now:

* uncertain demolished sites
* inferred “former” locations
* buried infrastructure
* vague “site of” discoveries
* prose-derived landmarks
  should lean heavily toward INTERPRETIVE_OVERLAY unless strongly grounded.

We are intentionally prioritizing trust over density.

3. Strengthen Walk Mode queue validation
   Please harden the queue/playback lifecycle:

* re-read the current place from placesRef by ID
* not rely solely on the original object captured during pickNext
* verify current discoveryClass before enqueueNarration

Goal:
A place downgraded after initial selection should not still narrate because of stale object state.

   Right now, once something becomes narrated, the original spatial concern is effectively hidden.

Example desired output:

  or

The goal is:
When a bad narration slips through, we can still understand WHY it should have been rejected.

5. Do NOT broaden discovery logic yet
   Please avoid:

* increasing density
* loosening thresholds
* adding fallback heuristics
* adding synthetic “interesting nearby” generation

We are intentionally narrowing and stabilizing the system first.

Success criteria:

* fewer narrated discoveries is acceptable
* silence is acceptable
* sparse neighborhoods are acceptable
* false positives are NOT acceptable

The current direction is improving. The important thing now is tightening trust boundaries and eliminating remaining bypass paths rather than expanding coverage.
